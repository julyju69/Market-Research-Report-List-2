<p><p>Antifouling paint for boats is a specialized type of coating applied to the hull of a boat to prevent the growth of organisms such as barnacles, algae, and mollusks. Antifouling paint contains biocides that prevent marine life from attaching to the hull.</p><p>The global antifouling paint for boats market is expected to grow at a rapid pace, with a projected CAGR of 13.1% during the forecast period. <p><p>Antifouling paint for boats is available in various types, including Self-Polishing Copolymer (SPC) coatings, Fouling Release Coatings (FRC), Ablative Type, and others. SPC coatings release biocides slowly over time to prevent fouling, while FRC coatings create a smooth surface that prevents organisms from attaching. Ablative type paints wear away over time, releasing biocides as they do. Other types of antifouling paints may include silicone or hybrid coatings, each offering unique benefits for boat owners looking to protect their vessel from marine growth.</p></p>
