<p><p>Aircraft tachometers are instruments used to measure the revolutions per minute (RPM) of an aircraft's engine. There are two main types of aircraft tachometers on the market: Analog type and Digital type. Analog tachometers use a traditional needle on a dial to display RPM readings, while Digital tachometers use electronic displays to show the RPM in numerical form. Both types of tachometers serve the same purpose of monitoring engine performance, but cater to different preferences for visual display.</p></p>
