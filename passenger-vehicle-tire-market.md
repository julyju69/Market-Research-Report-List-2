<p><ul><li>Radial Tire</li><li>Bias Tire</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Radial tires are constructed with layers of fabric cords running at right angles to the direction of travel, resulting in a more flexible tire that provides better grip, traction, and fuel efficiency. Bias tires have fabric cords running at an angle to the direction of travel, offering stiffer sidewalls and better stability, making them more suitable for heavy-duty vehicles like trucks. Both types have their own advantages and are used in different applications within the passenger vehicle tire market.</p></p>
