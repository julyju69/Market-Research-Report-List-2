<p><p>Ceramic heaters for semiconductor equipment come in various types, including Alumina (Al2O3) heaters, Silicon Nitride (Si3N4) heaters, and Aluminum Nitride (AlN) heaters. Alumina heaters are known for their high thermal conductivity and excellent electrical insulation properties. Silicon Nitride heaters are valued for their high-temperature strength and resistance to thermal shock. Aluminum Nitride heaters offer high thermal conductivity and excellent resistance to corrosion. Other ceramic heaters in the market may include materials like zirconia and boron nitride, each with their unique properties suited for specific semiconductor processing applications.</p></p>

<p><p>Ceramic heaters are commonly used in semiconductor equipment for various applications such as chemical vapor deposition (CVD), physical vapor deposition (PVD), etching, and other processes. These heaters provide precise and uniform heating required for the deposition and etching of semiconductor materials. In CVD, ceramic heaters are used to create the optimal temperature and environment for the deposition of thin films. In PVD, they are utilized for the evaporation of materials onto a substrate. Similarly, in etching processes, ceramic heaters play a crucial role in controlling the temperature for precise removal of material layers. Other applications in the semiconductor industry also rely on ceramic heaters for their heating requirements.</p></p>
