<p><p>Ceruloplasmin determination kits are used in the clinical setting to measure the levels of ceruloplasmin in blood samples. There are two main types of assays used for this purpose: nephelometry and immunoturbidimetry. Nephelometry measures the intensity of light scattered by particles in the sample, while immunoturbidimetry measures the change in absorbance when an antigen-antibody reaction occurs. Both methods are commonly used in the market for ceruloplasmin determination, providing accurate and efficient results for clinical diagnosis and monitoring of various health conditions.</p></p>
