<p><p>Drum brakes and disc brakes are two common types of brakes used in industrial machinery. Drum brakes use brake shoes to create friction against the inside of a drum to slow down or stop the machinery. On the other hand, disc brakes use pads to create friction against a disc to achieve the same result. Both types have their own advantages and limitations, and their market demand depends on factors such as the type of machinery, operating conditions, and performance requirements.</p></p>
