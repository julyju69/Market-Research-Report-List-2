<p><p>Dispersive prisms are optical devices used to separate light into its component colors. The 30°-60°-90° right angle prism is a type of dispersive prism with angles of 30, 60, and 90 degrees. It is commonly used in optical systems for reflecting and dispersing light. Equilateral prisms, on the other hand, have three equal 60-degree angles and are often used in spectroscopy and laser applications. Both types of prisms play a crucial role in various industries such as healthcare, telecommunications, and research.</p></p>
