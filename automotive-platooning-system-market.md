<p><p>Automotive platooning systems utilize Vehicle to Vehicle (V2V) communication and Vehicle to Infrastructure (V2I) communication to enable vehicles to communicate with each other and with road infrastructure. V2V communication allows vehicles in a platoon to exchange information such as speed, braking, and position to maintain a close-following distance safely. On the other hand, V2I communication involves vehicles interacting with traffic signals, signs, and other infrastructure to optimize traffic flow and safety. These technologies work together to enhance efficiency and safety on the road.</p></p>
