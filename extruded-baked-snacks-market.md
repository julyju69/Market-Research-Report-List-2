<p><p>Extruded baked snacks are produced using two main methods: hot extrusion and cold extrusion. Hot extrusion involves cooking the ingredients at high temperatures before shaping the snack, resulting in a crunchy texture and intense flavor. Cold extrusion, on the other hand, involves forming the snack at lower temperatures, resulting in a lighter, crispier texture. Both methods have their own unique market appeal, with hot extrusion snacks often favored for their bold flavors and cold extrusion snacks for their airy crunchiness.</p></p>
